ABOUT BONTANG KUALA

Nestled on the coast of Bontang, East Kalimantan, Bontang Kuala stands as a unique and captivating water village, offering a glimpse into the rich maritime heritage and cultural tapestry of the region. This bustling settlement, built predominantly on stilts over the tranquil waters of the Makassar Strait, is not only a picturesque floating community but also a burgeoning tourist destination recognized as a "Desa Wisata" (Tourism Village), pioneering the concept in East Kalimantan.

The origins of Bontang Kuala are deeply intertwined with the seafaring traditions of Indonesia. The village was primarily established by migrants, with the Bugis and Bajau people from Sulawesi playing a pivotal role in its foundation. Drawn to the rich marine resources of the area, these skilled seafarers and fishermen built their homes over the water, creating a settlement that harmonized with their way of life. Over time, other ethnic groups, including the local Kutai people, have contributed to the diverse and harmonious community that characterizes Bontang Kuala today, creating a unique "mixed diaspora culture." This blend of influences is a hallmark of the village's identity.

"The government always promotes Bontang Kuala as one of Bontang's tourist destinations. But there hasn't been a serious effort to develop it... This is the oldest area in Bontang, as well as a tourist spot, yet its condition is very concerning."

Bontang City Council, Abdul Samad

FYI! For your visit to Bontang Kuala, please know that you will first fly into Balikpapan (BPN) and then take a 4-6 hour overland trip to reach the village. Be ready to explore this unique floating community entirely on foot via its network of wooden boardwalks, so wear comfortable, non-slip shoes and bring sufficient cash for the delightful local food stalls. To respect the friendly local culture, modest dress is highly recommended for all visitors. Planning your trip during the dry season from June to September will offer the best weather to fully appreciate the charm and hospitality of this historic seaside settlement.
